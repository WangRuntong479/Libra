Origin of Mother's Day

Mother's Day was first celebrated in 1908 by Anna Jarvis. It became an official holiday in the United States in 1914 to honor mothers' love and sacrifices.

Global Traditions

  • USA: Sending flowers and cards
  • China: Gifting lilies to mothers
  • Mexico: Family celebrations with music

Significance of Mother's Day

A special day to:

  • Express gratitude to mothers
  • Celebrate unconditional love
  • Strengthen family bonds
